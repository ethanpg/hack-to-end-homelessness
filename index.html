So, here's what happened:

That social network for the homeless community? We built it.

An app that helps homeless youth in Seattle find shelter? Check.

Data on the causes of homelessness and what can solve it? We didn't just mine and visualize it, we helped service providers improve future data collection.

This weekend, we built something that matters: a community empowered by technology & design thinking to solve the problem of homelessness together. Thank you for joining us, and please check back here this week for more project updates.

Details:

Between May 1 and 4, 2014, we will host a series of events at Pioneer Square's Impact Hub:

  • Thursday, May 1: First Thursday ArtWalk in Pioneer Square with featured art at Impact Hub.
      • Featuring photos from Dan Lamont's Homeless in Washington series, photos from University District Youth Center program participants curated by Michael B. Maine, and portraits from Rex Hohlbein's Facing Homelessness.

  • Friday, May 2, 7:00 pm: Seattle premiere screening of @home.
      • Featuring reception by La Bodega and post-film discussion director Susanne Suffredin, Mark Horvath, subject of film and creator of InvisiblePeople.tv, and Mark Putnam of the Committee to End Homelessness in King County.

  • Saturday and Sunday, May 3-4: Hackathon for technologists, designers, innovators, and leading non-profits. More information at our Participant FAQ.
  • Sunday, May 4, 5:00 pm: Capstone presentations and reception, with panel featuring:
      • Kollin Min, Senior Program Officer, Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation
      Rebecca Lovell, Startup Liaison, City of Seattle
      Mike Mathieu, founder of FrontSeat and WalkScore
      Judy Lightfoot, Reporter, Crosscut
      Tim Harris, Executive Director, Real Change

Impact Hub Seattle is located at 220 2nd Ave S, Seattle, WA.
